A breakdown of contracts grouped by Global Vendor Name/DUNS number. Names of companies were cleaned with Open Refine.

# summarize data by top 30 vendors
HUBsum <- HUBZoneRaw %>% group_by(`Global Vendor Name`) %>%
  summarise(
    AvgContract = mean(`Action Obligation ($)`, na.rm = TRUE), 
    MedianContract = median(`Action Obligation ($)`, na.rm = TRUE),
    TotalContract = sum(`Action Obligation ($)`, na.rm = TRUE),
  )  %>%
  top_n(15)

Top Earners

Data shows a noteable gap between the top ten earners and the remaining firms in the District. According to figures, the top to firms were awarded more than 70 percent of all federal HUBZone dollars.

library(ggplot2)
# Graph data and reorder top 15 vendors
ggplot(HUBsum, aes(x = HUBsum$TotalContract, y = reorder(HUBsum$'Global Vendor Name', HUBsum$TotalContract)  )) +
  geom_point() +
  labs( x = "Contract Amounts", y = "Vendors")

Data shows the growth of contracts by year. Data shows that 2015 was the largest year for contracts in the District; followed by 2016 and 2017. Awards in 2018 were the lowest since 2007.

library(ggplot2)

# graphing by year
